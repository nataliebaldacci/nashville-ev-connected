Drive Electric Tennessee Initiative

TVA is working to bring 200,000 EVs onto Tennessee roads by 2028, which will create over 14,000 EV-related jobs, reduce carbon emissions by almost 1 million metric tons per year, reinvest $120 million in the local economy annually, and save drivers up to $1,000 in fuel and maintenance costs every year.

Major Milestones & Events
January 2019
Milestone
Drive Electric Tennessee Roadmap Released
Drive Electric Tennessee releases roadmap setting goal of 200,000 EVs on Tennessee roads by 2028. The initiative brings together state agencies, universities, cities, utilities, EV manufacturers, and advocacy groups.
Source: TN.gov EVSE Needs Assessment
September 2022
Infrastructure
45% of Nashville EVCI Down or Damaged
Metro General Services reports approximately 45% of Nashville's existing EV charging infrastructure is down or damaged, highlighting the need for replacement and upgrades.
Source: Electrify MUSIC City CFI Grant Application
June 2023
Grant
Electrify MUSIC City CFI Grant Application
Metro Nashville applies for Charging and Fueling Infrastructure (CFI) Discretionary Grant to replace, upgrade, and install 78 dual-port chargers across 34 locations, including 5 DC Fast Chargers. Project targets 63% of charging in disadvantaged communities.
Source: RS2023-2302
August 2023
Plan
NES Electric Vehicle Strategic Plan
Nashville Electric Service presents EV Strategic Plan to IEEE Nashville, outlining utility infrastructure planning for EV adoption including transformer capacity, rate schedules, and grid impacts.
Source: NES IEEE Presentation
2023
Infrastructure
NES & TVA Fast Chargers at Serra Chevrolet
NES and TVA unveil two new DC fast chargers at Serra Chevrolet Buick GMC in Madison. This is the first of five planned sites in Metro Nashville as part of TVA's EV infrastructure initiative.
Source: NES Power News
January 31, 2024
State Grant
$21M TEVI Awards for Statewide Charging
TDOT and TDEC announce $21 million in federal NEVI funds for 31 EV fast-charging stations across Tennessee's Alternative Fuel Corridors. Tennessee allocated $88 million over five years under the NEVI program.
Source: TDOT Press Release
September 16, 2024
Federal Grant
$4.7M CFI Grant Awarded to Metro Nashville
USDOT and FHWA award $4.7 million CFI grant to Metro Nashville for Electrify MUSIC City Project. Grant will double charging locations in Nashville with 63% in historically underinvested areas.
Source: NES Power News, Nashville.gov
October 21, 2025
Legislation
BL2025-958: EV Charging Facility Zoning Passed
Metro Council passes ordinance creating "Electric Vehicle Charging Facility" as a new principal use in commercial and industrial zoning districts. First Nashville zoning code to explicitly address standalone EV charging businesses.
Source: Nashville Legistar
January 20, 2026
Pending
BL2025-1116: Accessory EV Charging Stations
Ordinance on Council agenda to add "Electric Vehicle Charging Station" as accessory utility use in all zoning districts. Level 2 permitted everywhere; DCFC with conditions. Planning Commission recommended approval January 8, 2026.
Source: Nashville Legistar
Key Nashville EV Legislation
BL2025-958
Electric Vehicle Charging Facility
Passed Oct 21, 2025
Creates "Electric Vehicle Charging Facility" as principal commercial use. Permitted in CS, CA, CF, SCC, SCR, IWD, IR, IG zones. Allows standalone charging businesses (like Tesla Supercharger plazas).
View on Legistar
BL2025-1116
Electric Vehicle Charging Station (Accessory)
On Agenda Jan 20, 2026
Creates "Electric Vehicle Charging Station" as accessory utility use. Level 2: all zones. DCFC: all zones except AR2a, RS, R. Allows businesses to add chargers to existing parking.
View on Legistar
