Getting Started with EV Charging

A step-by-step guide to installing EV charging in Nashville

1
Check Your Eligibility

Before planning your installation, verify that your property can accommodate EV charging equipment.

  • Enter your address to check zoning requirements
  • Review what charger types are allowed at your property
  • Understand any restrictions or special requirements
Check Eligibility â†’
2
Assess Your Electrical System

Most Level 2 chargers require a 240V circuit. You'll need to evaluate your current electrical capacity.

  • Check your electrical panel capacity (most homes have 100-200 amp panels)
  • Determine available capacity for a new 40-50 amp circuit
  • Consider the distance from panel to charging location

If your panel is under 100 amps or has limited available capacity, you may need a panel upgrade. NES can help assess your service needs.

3
Understand Costs

Installation costs vary based on your specific situation. Here's what to budget for:

  • Level 2 charger equipment: $300-$700 (basic) or $500-$1,500 (smart chargers)
  • Installation labor: $500-$2,000 depending on complexity
  • Permits: $75-$150 for electrical permits
  • Panel upgrade (if needed): $1,500-$3,000+

4
Find a Contractor

Hire a licensed electrician with EV charging installation experience.

  • Review contractor rankings based on permit data
  • Get multiple quotes (we recommend 2-3)
  • Verify licensing with TN Contractor Board
  • Ask about warranty and support

5
Permits & Installation

Your contractor typically handles the permit process, but here's what to expect:

  • Electrical permit application (contractor submits)
  • Installation typically takes 2-4 hours
  • Metro Codes inspection after installation
  • Final approval and certificate of completion
