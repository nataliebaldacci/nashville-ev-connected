National Transformer Shortage

The U.S. is experiencing a significant transformer shortage with lead times of 1-3 years. This may affect electrical service upgrades required for commercial EV charging installations. Residential Level 2 chargers (typically 40-50 amps) usually do not require transformer upgrades, but commercial DC fast chargers often do. Contact NES early in your planning process to assess infrastructure requirements.

Feasibility by Installation Type

Residential Level 2 (240V, 40-50A)

Most homes can support without upgrades. Typical cost: $1,500-$3,500

Multi-Family Level 2 (Multiple Units)

May require panel upgrade or dedicated circuit. Cost: $3,000-$10,000 per station

Commercial Level 2 (Multiple Ports)

Often requires service upgrade. Contact NES for assessment. Cost: $5,000-$15,000

DC Fast Charging (50-350kW)

Requires significant infrastructure. Transformer may be needed. Cost: $50,000-$150,000+

How to Get an EV Charger Permit in Nashville

Installing an electric vehicle charging station requires an electrical permit from Metro Nashville Codes Department.

"EV" Address Designation

Nashville uses "EV" in addresses (e.g., "2600 EV JESS NEELY DR") to designate properties with approved EV charging. Our data shows 65 permits with this designation.

1

Hire a Licensed Electrical Contractor

Tennessee requires licensed contractors for electrical work.

2

Apply for Electrical Permit

Contractors submit through Nashville's online portal.

3

Installation & Inspection

Inspector verifies code compliance before activation.

4

Final Approval

Status changes to "DONE" and charging can begin!
