EV Charging Zoning in Nashville

Understanding where and how EV charging stations can be installed under Nashville's zoning code. Two new ordinances are reshaping the regulatory landscape for EV infrastructure.

The Key Distinction: Facility vs. Station

Nashville now has two separate zoning categories for EV charging. Understanding the difference is critical for permitting:

Electric Vehicle Charging Facility

BL2025-958
Passed Oct 21, 2025

Principal Use: Charging is the primary business purpose. The entire site is dedicated to EV charging operations.

  • Standalone charging locations
  • Dedicated charging depots
  • Fleet charging facilities
  • 24/7 operation permitted
  • Limited to commercial/industrial zones
Analogy: A gas station. Fueling is why the business exists.

Electric Vehicle Charging Station

BL2025-1116
On Agenda Jan 20, 2026

Accessory Use: Charging supports another principal use. The chargers serve an existing building or parking facility.

  • Office building parking garages
  • Apartment/condo complexes
  • Retail parking lots
  • Hotels, hospitals, schools
  • Permitted in almost all zones
Analogy: Gas pumps at a Costco. Fueling supports the main business.

Complete Framework

Together, these ordinances give Nashville a complete regulatory framework: you can either build charging as your primary business model (Facility) or add it to existing development (Station). Nashville didn't have either option until 2025.

Level 2 EVSE (3kW - 22kW)

  • Permitted in all zoning districts
  • Permitted wherever vehicular parking is allowed
  • Stalls count toward parking minimums
  • No screening requirements
  • No hour restrictions

  DC Fast Charging (50kW+)

  • Permitted in all zones except:
  • AR2a (Agricultural/Residential)
  • RS (Residential Single-Family)
  • R (Residential)
  • Must be 20 ft from AR2a, RS, R, RM zones
  • Stalls do NOT count toward parking minimums

  RM District Restrictions (Multifamily)

Within RM zoning districts, DCFC use is restricted to residents, guests, and employees of multifamily uses. Operation limited to 7 a.m. to 11 p.m. only.

Definitions (Proposed)

Key Terms from BL2025-1116

"Electric Vehicle Charging Station" means a site or facility that is accessory to another principal use and contains one or more vehicle stalls for the charging of electric vehicles (EVs) using electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE) and associated necessary infrastructure.

"Level 2 EVSE" means EVSE that utilizes alternating current and has an output power range of 3kW - 22kW.

"Direct Current Fast Charging (DCFC) EVSE" means EVSE that utilizes direct current and has an output power range of 50kW or greater.

Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need zoning approval before getting a building permit? +

Yes. Metro Codes Zoning Division reviews projects for zoning compliance before building permits are issued. You'll pay a $25 Zoning Examination fee as part of the permit application. If your project meets zoning by-right, approval is usually quick (a few days). If you need a variance or special exception, expect 4-8+ weeks for a BZA hearing.

Can I install a DC Fast Charger at my apartment complex? +

If BL2025-1116 passes, yes, with conditions. In RM (multifamily) zones, DCFC use is restricted to residents, guests, and employees only, and operation is limited to 7 a.m. to 11 p.m. The DCFC must also be at least 20 feet from any adjacent AR2a, RS, R, or RM property, and C-5 screening may be required.

What's the difference between a "Facility" and a "Station"? +

Facility (BL2025-958): Principal use. Charging is the primary business (like a Tesla Supercharger plaza). Only in commercial/industrial zones.

Station (BL2025-1116): Accessory use. Charging supports another primary use (like chargers in a grocery store parking lot). Permitted in almost all zones.

Can EV charging stalls count toward my parking minimums? +

Under BL2025-1116, Level 2 stalls can count toward parking minimums. DCFC stalls cannot. This is because Level 2 charging typically takes 4-8 hours (similar to regular parking duration), while DCFC is meant for quick 20-30 minute stops and shouldn't displace long-term parking.

What fire code requirements apply to EV chargers? +

Per BL2025-1004 (2024 IFC adoption), EV charging stations must comply with NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code) and include emergency shutoff devices that are clearly identified, readily accessible, marked "ELECTRIC VEHICLE EMERGENCY DISCONNECT," and disconnect all ungrounded conductors simultaneously.

My property is in a Specific Plan (SP) or PUD. What do I need? +

Properties in SP or PUD overlays may need amendment or Planning Department sign-off to add EV charging. The base zoning rules (BL2025-958/1116) apply, but your SP/PUD conditions may impose additional requirements. Contact Metro Planning at nashville.gov/departments/planning for guidance.

What's a C-5 buffer yard? +

A C-5 buffer yard is a landscaping requirement defined in Metro Code Â§17.24.240C. It's a type of screening that includes plantings and/or fencing to visually separate uses. Required for DCFC stations within 100 feet of residential zones under BL2025-1116, but waivable if the charging station is already screened by a structure or not visible from adjacent property.
